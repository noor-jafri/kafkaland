# 06 · Tax ID & Tax Classes

> **Goal of this stage:** Get your **Steuer-ID**, understand the tax-class system, and avoid being over-taxed in your first months.

Germany's tax admin is a top source of confusion for newcomers. Two things you must understand: your **Tax ID** and your **tax class (*Steuerklasse*)**.

---

## Your Tax ID (Steuer-Identifikationsnummer)

- An **11-digit** number issued **automatically** by the Federal Central Tax Office (**BZSt**) after your [Anmeldung](03-anmeldung.md).
- Arrives **by post**, typically in **2–4 weeks** (up to 6–8 weeks in busy periods).
- **Permanent** — it stays with you for life, even if you move.
- Your employer needs it to run payroll correctly.

> ⚠️ **Until your employer has your Tax ID**, payroll defaults to **tax class VI — the highest-deduction class** — so you're **over-taxed** at first. You reclaim the difference via your annual tax return (*Steuererklärung*). Give your employer your Steuer-ID as soon as it arrives.

**Don't confuse it with:**
- **Steuernummer** — a separate tax number issued by your local tax office, mainly for freelancers/businesses.
- **Sozialversicherungsnummer** — your social-security/pension number.

---

## The six tax classes (Steuerklassen)

Your class determines how much income tax is withheld from each paycheck. It depends on marital status and family situation:

| Class | Who | Typical situation |
|-------|-----|-------------------|
| **I** | Single / divorced / widowed | Most single employees |
| **II** | Single parents | Extra relief for single parents |
| **III** | Married, higher earner | Paired with class V spouse |
| **IV** | Married, similar incomes | Both spouses class IV |
| **IV + factor** | Married, fairer split | Optional factor method |
| **V** | Married, lower earner | Paired with class III spouse |
| **VI** | Second job / no Tax ID yet | Highest deductions |

### The III/V trap for couples

The married **III/V** combination gives the **higher earner (class III)** the tax-free allowances while the **lower earner (class V)** — often the accompanying immigrant spouse — sees **very high deductions** at the withholding stage. It balances out via a **mandatory joint annual return**, but the monthly cash flow feels unfair.

- **Alternatives:** switch both spouses to **IV**, or **IV + factor** for a fairer monthly split.
- A planned reform to **abolish classes III and V** (moving couples to IV with a factor) was slated for **2030** but was **dropped** from the final law enacted in December 2024 — so **III/V remains in force**.

---

## The annual tax return (Steuererklärung)

- Filing is often **worth it** — many employees get refunds (over-withholding, class VI at the start, work-related deductions).
- Deadlines apply; using software (e.g. ELSTER, or English-friendly tools) helps.
- Employees whose only income is one salary in class I are sometimes not required to file, but usually benefit from doing so.

---

## ✅ Checklist

- [ ] Anmeldung done → waiting for Tax ID by post
- [ ] Tax ID received and given to employer (stops class VI over-taxation)
- [ ] Understand your tax class (and III/V vs IV/IV if married)
- [ ] Plan to file an annual return to reclaim over-withheld tax
