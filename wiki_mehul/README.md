# 🇩🇪 Moving to Germany: A Step-by-Step Wiki

*From landing at the airport to signing your first employment contract.*

This wiki breaks the journey of immigrating to Germany into discrete, ordered steps — one file per stage. It is written for **non-EU skilled workers, students, and family members**, though EU citizens and refugees will find much of it useful too (differences are flagged where they matter).

> ⚠️ **Reality check.** In the InterNations *Expat Insider 2025* survey, internationals ranked Germany **42nd of 46 countries overall** and **dead last (46th) on "Expat Essentials"** (bureaucracy, housing, digital life, language). This wiki exists because the process is genuinely hard — but it is navigable if you do things in the right order. See the full [research report](../) this wiki is based on.

---

## 🗺️ The journey at a glance

The path is **not** perfectly linear — several steps depend on each other and are done in a cluster during your first weeks. Here's the realistic flow:

```
BEFORE YOU LEAVE
  01. Documents, apostilles, degree pre-check, learn some German
        │
  02. Apply for the right visa (Blue Card / Chancenkarte / student / family …)
        │
   ✈️  ARRIVE IN GERMANY
        │
  ┌─────────────────── FIRST 2–4 WEEKS (the "cluster") ───────────────────┐
  │  03. Find temporary housing → 08. Hunt for a permanent flat           │
  │  04. Anmeldung (register your address)  ←── unlocks almost everything  │
  │  05. Open a bank account   06. Get your Tax ID   07. Health insurance  │
  └───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
        │
  09. Convert your visa → residence permit at the Ausländerbehörde
        │
  10. Learn German / integration course   11. Recognise your qualifications
        │
  12. Job search, applications, interviews  →  🎯 EMPLOYMENT CONTRACT
        │
  13. Everyday life admin (driving licence, TV fee, recycling …)
        │
  14. Path to permanent residence & citizenship
```

## ✅ How to use this wiki

1. **Read [01](01-before-you-arrive.md) and [02](02-visa-and-entry.md) months before you move.** The visa and document steps have the longest lead times.
2. **Bookmark [03 Anmeldung](03-anmeldung.md).** It is the keystone: without it you can't easily get a permit, a proper bank account, or a tax ID.
3. **Keep [14 Glossary](14-glossary-and-resources.md) open** — German bureaucratic vocabulary is half the battle.
4. Each page ends with a **navigation bar** (← previous · home · next →) and a **"Sources"** section.

## ⚖️ Disclaimer

This is a practical orientation guide, not legal advice. Immigration, tax, and citizenship rules in Germany changed repeatedly in 2023–2026 and vary by federal state (*Bundesland*) and city. **Always verify against official sources** — [Make it in Germany](https://www.make-it-in-germany.com/en/), [BAMF](https://www.bamf.de/EN/), and your local *Ausländerbehörde* — before acting. Figures are current as of mid-2026.
