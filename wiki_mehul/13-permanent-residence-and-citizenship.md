# 13 · Permanent Residence & Citizenship

> **Goal of this stage:** Move from temporary permits to **permanent residence (*Niederlassungserlaubnis*)** and, eventually, **German citizenship** — with an eye on rules that changed sharply in 2024–2025.

Once you're settled and working, the long game is **security of status**. There are two milestones: **permanent residence** (indefinite right to stay) and **naturalisation** (citizenship).

---

## Permanent residence (Niederlassungserlaubnis)

An indefinite residence permit — you no longer renew, and job-change restrictions ease.

| Route | Time to qualify | Language | Notes |
|-------|-----------------|----------|-------|
| **EU Blue Card holder** | **27 months** (or **21 months** with B1) | A1 (27 mo) / B1 (21 mo) | Fastest route; pension contributions throughout |
| **Skilled worker** (§18a/b) | **3 years** (down from 4) | B1 | 36 months of pension contributions (was 48) |
| **Skilled worker with German degree** | **2 years / 24 months** | B1 | Even faster |
| **General standard route** (§9) | **5 years** | B1 | 60 months pension + adequate housing, no reliance on *Bürgergeld* |

Common requirements across routes: **secure livelihood**, **pension contributions**, **adequate housing**, **no serious criminal record**, and usually **B1 German** (A1 for the fast Blue Card track).

---

## Citizenship (naturalisation) — a moving target

The rules were **liberalised in 2024** and then **partly reversed in 2025**. Here's the current picture:

### The 2024 reform (in force 27 June 2024)
- **Dual citizenship generally permitted** for the first time — you no longer have to give up your original nationality.
- Standard naturalisation residence requirement **cut from 8 to 5 years**.
- A **3-year fast track** was introduced for exceptional integration (**C1 German** + special achievements).

### The 2025 reversal (in force 30 October 2025)
- Following the 2025 change of government (CDU/CSU–SPD), the Bundestag **abolished the 3-year fast track** on 8 October 2025.
- A uniform **5-year minimum** was restored.
- **Dual citizenship and the 5-year path remain.**
- The reversal even affected **pending applications** filed under the 3-year rule — some were put on hold or rejected.

> ⚠️ **Lesson:** immigration rules in Germany can change fast and even affect applications already in progress. Track official updates and, for citizenship, get current advice before applying.

### Typical citizenship requirements (5-year route)
- 5 years' lawful residence
- **B1 German** (higher for any special routes)
- **"Leben in Deutschland" / naturalisation test** passed
- Secure livelihood without reliance on state benefits
- Commitment to the free democratic constitutional order
- Clean criminal record

---

## Why this matters

- **Permanent residence** removes the renewal treadmill and permit-tied-to-employer anxiety.
- **Citizenship** adds voting rights, an EU passport, freedom of movement across the EU, and full security of status.
- Germany's naturalisation rate has historically been **below the EU average**, with **12+ million residents** lacking citizenship — the 2024 reform aimed to change that, though 2025 tightened it again.

## ✅ Checklist

- [ ] Tracking your qualifying time toward permanent residence
- [ ] Pension contributions being paid (and documented)
- [ ] German at **B1** (or A1 for fast Blue Card PR)
- [ ] Adequate housing + no reliance on Bürgergeld
- [ ] For citizenship: 5 years' residence, B1+, naturalisation test planned
- [ ] Monitoring rule changes before applying
