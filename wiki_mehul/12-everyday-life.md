# 12 · Everyday Life Admin

> **Goal of this stage:** Handle the smaller-but-mandatory bits of German daily life that trip up newcomers — driving licence, the TV fee, recycling, and cultural rhythms.

Once the big steps are done, a cluster of everyday rules remains. None is huge alone, but ignoring them causes fines and friction.

---

## 🚗 Driving licence

- A **non-EU/EEA licence is valid for only 6 months** after you establish residence (§29 FeV). After that you must **exchange it** (*Umschreibung*).
- Whether you need a **test** depends on bilateral agreements (Annex 11 FeV):
  - **Listed countries** (e.g. UK, ~39 US states on "List A") — exchange **without** a test.
  - **Other countries** — require full **theory + practical tests**, plus an **eye test** and **first-aid course**.
- You'll typically need a **certified translation** of your licence.
- **EU/EEA licences** are valid and simply swapped when they expire.

> 💡 If you're from a "test required" country, start the process early — driving schools and test slots take time.

---

## 📺 Rundfunkbeitrag (broadcasting fee)

- A **mandatory public-broadcasting fee** charged **per household**, regardless of whether you own a TV or watch anything.
- Currently around **€18.36/month** (verify current rate) — one payment covers the whole household.
- You'll be contacted after your [Anmeldung](03-anmeldung.md); register your household and pay to avoid penalties.
- Flatmates in a shared flat only pay **once per household**, not per person.

## ♻️ Waste separation & recycling

Germany takes recycling seriously, and neighbours will notice mistakes:

- **Restmüll** (grey/black) — general waste
- **Papier** (blue) — paper & cardboard
- **Bio** (brown) — organic/food waste
- **Gelber Sack / Gelbe Tonne** (yellow) — packaging, plastics, metals
- **Glas** — sorted by colour (white/green/brown), dropped at public bins
- **Pfand** — deposit on many bottles/cans; return them at supermarket machines for cash back

## 🕐 Cultural rhythms that surprise newcomers

- **Sundays are closed.** Almost all shops shut on Sundays and public holidays (*Ruhetag*). Stock up on Saturday; only stations, bakeries, and some kiosks open.
- **Quiet hours (*Ruhezeit*)** — no loud noise typically 22:00–06:00 and often all day Sunday (no drilling, loud music, vacuuming in some buildings).
- **Cash culture** — carry cash; many small businesses don't take cards ([Step 05](05-bank-account.md)).
- **Punctuality & directness** — being on time is respect; German directness isn't rudeness.
- **Appointments for everything** — offices, doctors, even some shops expect a *Termin*.

---

## Other practical bits

- **Mobile & internet:** contracts (*Vertrag*) vs. prepaid; contracts often want a Meldebescheinigung + bank account. Internet installation can take weeks — arrange early.
- **Liability insurance (*Haftpflichtversicherung*)** — not mandatory but near-universal and cheap; strongly recommended.
- **Registering a dog, car (*Kfz-Zulassung*), etc.** — each has its own office and fee.
