# 07 · Health Insurance

> **Goal of this stage:** Enrol in mandatory health insurance and register with a doctor — ideally before you're sick.

Health insurance is **mandatory** in Germany, and you generally can't complete your [residence permit](08-residence-permit.md) or start a job without it. The system splits into **public (GKV)** and **private (PKV)**.

---

## Public (GKV) vs. Private (PKV)

| | **GKV (Gesetzliche Krankenversicherung)** | **PKV (Private Krankenversicherung)** |
|---|---|---|
| **Who** | Most employees (mandatory below an income threshold) | High earners above the threshold, freelancers, some students |
| **Cost** | ~% of gross income, shared with employer; family members often covered free | Risk-based premiums; each person pays separately |
| **Providers** | TK, AOK, Barmer, DAK, etc. | Allianz, DKV, etc. |
| **Best for** | Predictable, family coverage, most newcomers | Young, healthy, high earners; some faster access |
| **Switching back** | — | Hard to switch back to GKV later — decide carefully |

> 💡 Most employed immigrants go with **GKV**. **TK** and other big funds have English-language service and are popular with internationals.

**Students** typically enrol in student GKV rates or need proof of equivalent coverage.

---

## Finding a doctor (harder than it sounds)

- Your first stop is a **Hausarzt** (GP / family doctor), who refers you to specialists.
- Many practices **cap the number of public (GKV) patients** they accept, because public insurance pays doctors a flat quarterly rate — so "accepting new patients" can be a struggle.
- **English-speaking doctors** exist but are concentrated in big cities and fill up.

**Tools:**
- **Doctolib** and **Jameda** let you filter by **language** and **insurance type** and book online.
- The **116117** service and *Terminservicestellen* help arrange appointments.

---

## Wait times

- In 2024, statutory-insured patients waited an average of **42 days** for a specialist appointment (up from 33 days in 2019).
- By law, GKV patients should get a specialist appointment within **4 weeks** of referral — but "new patient" slots are a known bottleneck.
- For urgent issues, use *Bereitschaftsdienst* (116117) or, for emergencies, 112.

---

## Mental health

Support gaps are notable, especially for immigrants:
- Therapy waitlists can be long.
- English-speaking therapists are scarce and often private-pay.
- NaDiRa research links repeated experiences of discrimination with higher psychological stress — if you're struggling, seek support early through your insurer, university, or expat networks.

*This is a sensitive area; if you're in distress, your GP, insurer, or local services can help you find the right support.*

---

## ✅ Checklist

- [ ] Chosen GKV (most people) or PKV — understand it's hard to reverse PKV
- [ ] Enrolled and received insurance card (*Gesundheitskarte*)
- [ ] Registered with a **Hausarzt** near you
- [ ] Doctolib/Jameda set up with language + insurance filters
- [ ] Know 116117 (non-emergency) and 112 (emergency)
