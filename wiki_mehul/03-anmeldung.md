# 03 · Anmeldung — Address Registration

> **Goal of this stage:** Register your German address at the *Bürgeramt* to receive your **Meldebescheinigung** — the document that unlocks almost everything else.

The **Anmeldung** is the keystone of German life admin. It is a legal requirement to register your address within **~2 weeks** of moving in, and the certificate it produces (**Meldebescheinigung**) is demanded by banks, employers, the tax office, and the [Ausländerbehörde](08-residence-permit.md).

---

## The chicken-and-egg problem

This is the single most frustrating catch newcomers hit:

```
        You need an ADDRESS to do your Anmeldung
                        ↕
   You often need a Meldebescheinigung to rent a flat,
        open a proper bank account, sign a contract…
```

**How people break the loop:**
- Register at a **temporary address** first — a landlord, friend, or serviced apartment whose owner will sign your **Wohnungsgeberbestätigung** (landlord confirmation).
- Some Airbnb/temporary-housing providers (e.g. Wunderflats) explicitly offer Anmeldung-friendly contracts.
- Use a **neobank** (N26/Wise/Revolut) that doesn't require Anmeldung to get banking running in parallel — see [Step 05](05-bank-account.md).

---

## What you need to bring

- Valid **passport** (and visa)
- Completed **Anmeldung form** (*Anmeldung bei der Meldebehörde*) — usually German-only
- **Wohnungsgeberbestätigung** — signed confirmation from your landlord/host that you live there (mandatory)
- Marriage/birth certificates if registering a family

---

## How to do it

1. **Find your local Bürgeramt / Einwohnermeldeamt** (citizens' office). In big cities this is borough-based.
2. **Book an appointment online.** In Berlin, Munich, Hamburg, and Frankfurt, appointments can be **booked out for weeks** — check daily for cancellations, or try offices in less busy districts.
3. Attend in person, submit your form + landlord confirmation.
4. Receive your **Meldebescheinigung** on the spot (usually free or a few euros).
5. Your **Tax ID (Steuer-ID)** is then generated automatically and posted to you within 2–8 weeks — see [Step 06](06-tax-id-and-tax-classes.md).

> 💡 **Tip:** Photograph and store several copies of the Meldebescheinigung. You will be asked for it repeatedly, and offices often want a recent one.

---

## What the Anmeldung unlocks

| Unlocks | Why it matters |
|---------|----------------|
| **Tax ID (Steuer-ID)** | Needed for legal employment & correct tax |
| **Residence permit** | The Ausländerbehörde requires proof of registration |
| **Full bank account** | Traditional banks want a Meldebescheinigung |
| **Health insurance** | Registration & enrolment |
| **SIM cards, gym, contracts** | Most German contracts request it |

---

## ⚠️ Common pitfalls

- **Registering late** can technically incur a fine — do it within two weeks.
- **No Wohnungsgeberbestätigung = no Anmeldung.** Confirm your landlord will sign *before* you move in.
- **Sub-letting without permission** can leave you unable to register — verify your host is allowed to register tenants.
- Every time you **move**, you must re-register (*Ummeldung*).

---

## ✅ Checklist

- [ ] Temporary or permanent address secured
- [ ] Landlord confirmation (*Wohnungsgeberbestätigung*) signed
- [ ] Bürgeramt appointment booked
- [ ] Anmeldung completed → **Meldebescheinigung** in hand
- [ ] Waiting for Tax ID by post
