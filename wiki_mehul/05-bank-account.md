# 05 · Bank Account

> **Goal of this stage:** Get a German IBAN so you can receive salary, pay rent, and set up direct debits (*Lastschrift*) — which run almost everything here.

A German bank account (*Girokonto*) is essential: employers pay into it, landlords collect rent by direct debit, and most contracts (phone, insurance, gym) are paid this way.

---

## The catch-22

```
   Traditional banks often want a Meldebescheinigung (Anmeldung)
                        ↕
        …but you may need a bank account to rent a flat,
                 which you need to do Anmeldung
```

**The fix:** open a **neobank** account first (they usually don't require Anmeldung), then optionally add a traditional bank later.

---

## Neobanks vs. traditional banks

| | **Neobanks** (N26, Wise, Revolut) | **Traditional banks** (Sparkasse, Deutsche Bank, Commerzbank, ING) |
|---|---|---|
| **Anmeldung required?** | Usually **no** | Usually **yes** |
| **Setup** | App-based, minutes | Branch or video-ident, slower |
| **Language** | English-friendly | Often German-only |
| **Best for** | Getting started fast, arriving before you have an address | Long-term, in-person service, mortgages, SCHUFA-building |
| **SCHUFA** | Some run a check; Wise is often the most lenient | Usually run a SCHUFA check |

> 💡 A common strategy: **Wise or N26 on arrival** → salary + rent flowing → add a Sparkasse/ING account once you have your Meldebescheinigung and want fuller services.

---

## What you'll typically need

- Passport / ID
- **Meldebescheinigung** (for traditional banks)
- **Tax ID (Steuer-ID)** — see [Step 06](06-tax-id-and-tax-classes.md)
- Proof of employment or student status (sometimes)
- A German address (neobanks may accept a temporary one)

---

## SCHUFA and banking

Opening and responsibly using a German account is how you **start building a SCHUFA record** — which you'll later need for [housing](04-finding-housing.md), phone contracts, and loans. The lack of a SCHUFA history is a newcomer disadvantage, so getting banked early pays off.

---

## Cash culture note

Germany is historically **cash-heavy** — one reason it ranks poorly on "digital life." Card acceptance has improved, but:
- Carry cash for small shops, bakeries, markets, and some restaurants.
- Many places take only **Girocard (EC)**, not international credit cards.
- Set up **direct debit (*Lastschrift*)** and **standing orders (*Dauerauftrag*)** for recurring bills.

---

## ✅ Checklist

- [ ] Neobank account opened (works before Anmeldung)
- [ ] German IBAN received and given to employer/landlord
- [ ] Direct debits set up for rent & utilities
- [ ] (Optional) Traditional bank account added after Anmeldung
- [ ] Some cash on hand at all times
