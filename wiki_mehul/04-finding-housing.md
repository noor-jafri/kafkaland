# 04 · Finding Housing

> **Goal of this stage:** Move from temporary accommodation into a permanent flat — the hardest, most competitive part of settling in, especially in big cities.

Germany has its **largest housing deficit in over 20 years** — the Pestel Institute estimated a shortage of **more than 700,000 dwellings**, and **63% of expats** say housing is hard to find (vs. 36% globally). In Munich, Berlin, Frankfurt, and Hamburg, dozens of applicants compete for a single viewing.

---

## Cold rent vs. warm rent

- **Kaltmiete (cold rent):** base rent only.
- **Warmmiete (warm rent):** cold rent + *Nebenkosten* (utilities/service charges). **Always compare warm rents.**
- **Kaution (deposit):** capped by law at **3 months' cold rent**, held in a separate account and returned (minus damages) when you leave.

---

## What landlords ask for (your application dossier)

Assemble a single PDF "*Bewerbungsmappe*" to send instantly when you find a listing:

- **Copy of passport / ID**
- **Proof of income** — usually last 3 payslips, or an employment contract; landlords typically want income **≈ 3× the rent**
- **SCHUFA credit report** (see below)
- **Meldebescheinigung** from your [Anmeldung](03-anmeldung.md)
- **Self-disclosure form (*Selbstauskunft*)**
- Sometimes a **guarantor (*Bürge*)** or previous-landlord reference (*Mietschuldenfreiheitsbescheinigung*)

> 💡 Speed wins. Have the dossier ready **before** you start viewing.

---

## The SCHUFA problem for newcomers

**SCHUFA** is Germany's main credit-scoring agency. Landlords almost always want a **SCHUFA-BonitätsAuskunft**. As a newcomer you have **no SCHUFA history**, which puts you at a structural disadvantage. Workarounds:

- Offer additional deposit or prepayment where legal.
- Provide a guarantor.
- Use employer letters and bank statements to compensate.
- A SCHUFA record starts building once you have a German bank account and address.

---

## ⚠️ Discrimination is real and documented

- An **LMU Munich** study found applicants with **non-Western "ethnic" names** are disadvantaged in the rental market.
- The late-2025 **National Discrimination and Racism Monitor (NaDiRa)** housing report concluded that people marked by racism — **especially Black and Muslim people** — are systematically disadvantaged in access to housing, and that a **foreign-sounding name alone** can lower your chance of even getting a viewing.

**Practical responses:** apply widely, lead with your strongest documents, get German-speaking friends/colleagues to help, and use anti-discrimination advice centres (*Antidiskriminierungsstelle*) if you experience blatant discrimination.

---

## Where to search

- **Portals:** ImmoScout24, Immowelt, Kleinanzeigen, WG-Gesucht (for shared flats / *WGs*).
- **Shared flats (*Wohngemeinschaft / WG*)** are the fastest, cheapest entry point for newcomers and students.
- **Housing cooperatives (*Genossenschaften*)** and municipal housing can be cheaper but have long waitlists.
- **Company/relocation help** — ask your employer; many offer relocation support or temporary housing.

---

## 🚨 Avoiding rental scams

The tight market breeds scams targeting desperate, German-less newcomers:

- **Never pay a deposit before viewing** the flat in person (or via a verified video tour).
- Be suspicious of below-market rent, landlords "abroad," or requests to wire money via Western Union.
- Never hand over money for "keys by post."
- Verify the landlord's identity and that the property exists.
