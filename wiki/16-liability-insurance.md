# Liability Insurance (Haftpflichtversicherung)

> Last researched: 2026-07-23. Verify current rules/fees before relying on this, as German bureaucracy changes frequently.

**Sequence:** Side quest — not legally required, but strongly recommended in your first weeks.
**Prerequisites:** [05-bank-account.md](05-bank-account.md) (for SEPA payment setup).
**Unlocks:** Landlord confidence when apartment-hunting; protection against unlimited personal liability.

## Overview

Not legally required, but functionally close to mandatory in German culture — surveys consistently show around 80% of German households carry a personal liability policy (Privathaftpflichtversicherung), and many landlords/flatshares expect you to have one.

## Process

- Compare and buy online via comparison sites (Check24, Verivox) or directly from an insurer (HUK-Coburg, ADAC, Getsafe, Feather, etc.); can typically be set up and active within a day, no medical checks needed.
- Coverage kicks in for accidental damage you cause to other people or their property — e.g., breaking a friend's laptop, your kid damaging something at school, accidentally flooding a neighbor's flat.

## Costs (verify current figures)

- Roughly €3–7/month for an individual, slightly more for families — very cheap relative to the protection it provides.

## Common problems & issues

- Newcomers often don't realize German law (§823 BGB) makes you **personally and fully liable** for damage you cause, with no cap — a single accident (e.g., water damage to a neighbor's apartment) can run into tens of thousands of euros without insurance.
- Some landlords explicitly ask for proof of Haftpflicht coverage before signing a lease, which surprises people who've never encountered this in their home country.
- People coming from countries where liability coverage is bundled into home/renters insurance can wrongly assume they're covered when they're not — German Haftpflicht is a separate policy from Hausratversicherung (contents insurance).

## Benefits

- Extremely cheap relative to the unlimited liability it protects against.
- Fast to set up (often same-day, fully online, no medical checks).
- Can smooth apartment-hunting, since some landlords ask for proof of coverage.

## Drawbacks

- Easy to overlook since it's not legally mandated — the risk of skipping it is invisible until an accident happens.
- A separate policy from contents insurance (Hausrat), which can confuse people used to bundled home-insurance products elsewhere.

## Tips

- Get this early, ideally in your first weeks — it's cheap and low-effort, and the downside of not having it is unlimited.
- Keep proof of coverage handy when apartment-hunting; it's a small thing that can make a landlord more comfortable choosing you.
