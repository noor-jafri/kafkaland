# Radio Tax (Rundfunkbeitrag)

> Last researched: 2026-07-23. Verify current rules/fees before relying on this, as German bureaucracy changes frequently.

**Sequence:** Side quest — triggered automatically by Anmeldung, but easy to miss.
**Prerequisites:** [04-city-registration-anmeldung.md](04-city-registration-anmeldung.md).
**Unlocks:** Nothing further, but skipping it leads to backdated bills — see Common problems below.

## Overview

The Rundfunkbeitrag is Germany's mandatory household fee for public TV/radio broadcasting, and it is **not optional** — every household pays it regardless of whether you own a TV or radio.

## Process

- It is charged **per household** (Wohnung), not per person, so flatmates split it.
- Registration is often triggered automatically once you complete your Anmeldung (address registration), since the Bürgeramt shares your data with the Beitragsservice — but letters can take months to arrive, and the fee is backdated to your move-in date regardless.
- You can also proactively register yourself at rundfunkbeitrag.de rather than waiting for a letter.

## Costs (verify current figures)

- As of 2026 it is **€18.36/month per household** (all 16 states froze it at this rate through end of 2026) — verify current figure at rundfunkbeitrag.de.

## Common problems & issues

- **Backdated bills:** don't ignore the letter when it arrives, and don't assume "no letter yet" means you don't owe anything — proactively registering at rundfunkbeitrag.de avoids a surprise multi-month bill.
- Flatmates sometimes disagree over who registers/pays, since it's billed per household rather than per person — worth agreeing on this when you move in.

## Benefits

- Once registered and set up (usually via SEPA direct debit), it's a small, predictable, entirely automatic recurring cost — no further action needed.
- Splitting per household (not per person) means shared flats pay less per capita than solo renters.

## Drawbacks

- Mandatory regardless of whether you actually use public broadcasting — a flat, unavoidable household tax.
- The automatic trigger from Anmeldung can take months to result in a letter, but the fee still accrues from your move-in date, catching newcomers off guard.

## Tips

- Register proactively at rundfunkbeitrag.de rather than waiting for the letter — it removes the backdating surprise.
- Sort out who in the household is responsible for paying immediately after moving in together.
