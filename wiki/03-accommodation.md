# Accommodation in Nuremberg

> Last researched: 2026-07-23. Verify current rules/fees before relying on this, as German bureaucracy changes frequently.

**Prerequisites:** [02-arrival-and-first-days.md](02-arrival-and-first-days.md).

## Overview

Finding a place to live is the single hardest logistical hurdle for almost everyone moving to Nuremberg, regardless of visa type. This is true whether you're an EU citizen, a work-visa or EU Blue Card holder, a job seeker, a student, joining family, or coming on a freelance (self-employment) visa — Germany does not care why you're renting, only whether your paperwork and finances look solid on paper. Nuremberg's market is tight but noticeably less brutal than Munich, Berlin, or Frankfurt; still, competitive listings can draw 20-40+ applicants within days.

The core chicken-and-egg problem for newcomers: landlords want a German Schufa credit report and proof of a stable local income, but you can't build a Schufa history or often even get a bank account without a registered address (Anmeldung), and you can't register an address without a signed lease. Most people solve this by securing temporary/bridge housing first, then hunting for a permanent place once they have some paperwork established.

## Step-by-step process

1. **Prepare your application folder (Bewerbungsmappe) before you start searching.** Assemble: passport/ID copy, visa/residence permit copy (if applicable), Schufa-Auskunft or Bonitätsauskunft, last 3 payslips or employment contract/income proof, Mietschuldenfreiheitsbescheinigung from a previous landlord (or explanatory letter if coming from abroad), and a completed Mieterselbstauskunft (self-disclosure form).
2. **Book temporary/bridge accommodation first** if arriving from abroad — a serviced apartment (Wunderflats, Mr. Lodge, Airbnb monthly stay), a room via WG-Gesucht, a hostel, or short-term corporate housing. This buys time to search properly and gives you an address for early bureaucracy in some cases (though ideally you want a real lease for Anmeldung — see the Anmeldung wiki page).
3. **Search multiple portals simultaneously** and set up email/push alerts:
   - **ImmobilienScout24 (immobilienscout24.de)** — the dominant listings portal, covers apartments and shared rooms, filter by Kaltmiete, Bezirk (district), move-in date.
   - **WG-Gesucht (wg-gesucht.de)** — the go-to for shared flats (WGs) and temporary rooms; also lists full apartments. Popular with students and young professionals; a Basic membership is free but a paid "plus" membership (~€15-20/month, verify current figures) gets you faster access to new listings and unlocks messaging limits.
   - **eBay Kleinanzeigen (formerly eBay Kleinanzeigen/Classifieds)** — skews toward private landlords, sometimes fewer bureaucratic hoops, but also more scam risk.
   - **Facebook groups** — search "Nuremberg Expats," "Nürnberg Wohnungen," "WG Nürnberg," etc. Useful for word-of-mouth listings that never hit the big portals.
   - **wbg Nürnberg (wbg.nuernberg.de)** — the city's own housing association; manages both market-rate and subsidized (WBS) flats. Worth registering as an interested party even before you have a WBS if you qualify.
   - **Immowelt** — secondary portal with listings that often don't overlap with ImmoScout24, worth checking in parallel.
4. **Respond fast and apply with a complete folder.** For hot listings, message within minutes/hours of posting; attach your full Bewerbungsmappe immediately rather than waiting to be asked.
5. **Attend the viewing (Besichtigung).** In competitive listings, expect a "Massenbesichtigung" (mass viewing) where 10-40 people tour the same flat in one slot — dress reasonably, bring printed documents, and be ready to hand over your folder on the spot.
6. **Get selected and sign the Mietvertrag (rental contract).** Read it for: Kaltmiete (cold/base rent) vs. Warmmiete (rent + utilities), Nebenkosten (ancillary costs), Kaution (deposit) amount and payment terms, notice period, and whether a fitted kitchen (Einbauküche) is included or must be bought from the outgoing tenant.
7. **Pay the deposit (Kaution)** — typically wired to a dedicated deposit account before or at move-in.
8. **Do a move-in inspection (Wohnungsübergabeprotokoll)** with the landlord, documenting existing damage, meter readings, and key handover in writing/photos.
9. **Get the Wohnungsgeberbestätigung** (landlord confirmation of move-in) from your landlord immediately — you need this within days for your Anmeldung (see the separate Anmeldung wiki page).

## Documents/Requirements checklist

- Passport / national ID
- Visa or residence permit (if non-EU)
- Schufa-Auskunft or Bonitätsauskunft (credit report) — landlord-ready version costs roughly €29.95 via meineschufa.de (verify current figures); newcomers with no German credit history can substitute a foreign bank reference letter, recent bank statements, and a short explanatory cover note
- Proof of income: last 3 payslips (Gehaltsabrechnungen), employment contract, or employer confirmation letter; landlords generally want net income ≥ 3x the rent
- Self-employed/freelancers: last 1-2 tax assessments plus 6 months of bank statements in lieu of payslips
- Mietschuldenfreiheitsbescheinigung — a certificate from your previous landlord confirming no rent arrears (hard to produce if you're new to Germany; a written explanation or reference letter can substitute)
- Mieterselbstauskunft — a self-disclosure form covering household size, employment, pets, smoking, prior evictions, etc.; some landlords provide their own version
- WBS (Wohnberechtigungsschein) — only needed if pursuing subsidized housing (see below)
- For couples/families: marriage certificate, birth certificates of children if relevant to household size questions

## Costs (EUR, verify current figures)

- **Typical Kaltmiete (cold rent) in Nuremberg** (2026 approximate ranges, verify): unfurnished 2-room apartment roughly €570-€1,025/month; furnished 1-room apartment roughly €700-€1,350/month depending on district and furnishing.
- **By district (rough, furnished 1-bed, verify):** central/Altstadt and desirable northern districts (Erlenstegen, Mögeldorf) trend higher; Südstadt and outer districts trend more affordable, roughly €600-€1,150/month.
- **Historical reference point (2015 data, now outdated but shows relative positioning):** existing apartments ~€8.47/m², new builds ~€11.16/m², premium locations ~€14.50/m² — expect meaningfully higher figures today; check ImmoScout24's live price atlas for current numbers.
- **Nebenkosten (utilities/ancillary costs):** roughly €2.50-€3.20/m²/month on top of cold rent (electricity is separate and metered individually).
- **Kaution (deposit):** legally capped at 3 months' cold rent (§551 BGB); often payable in up to 3 installments. A Mietkautionsbürgschaft (deposit guarantee/insurance) is an alternative to a cash deposit, costing roughly 3-5% of the deposit amount per year, but not all landlords accept it.
- **Agent commission:** under the Bestellerprinzip, whoever hires the agent pays — tenants should generally not be charged a Makler fee for standard rentals.
- **WG-Gesucht premium membership:** roughly €15-20/month (verify current pricing) for faster access to listings.

## Timeline

- Budget **4-12 weeks** for a full apartment search in Nuremberg if you want a good long-term flat, longer in peak seasons (semester starts, September/October).
- Bridge/temporary housing bookings can be arranged within days to a couple of weeks.
- Viewing-to-decision cycle for a popular listing: landlords often decide within 24-72 hours after a mass viewing.
- Once you sign a lease, move-in is typically negotiated to start on the 1st or 15th of a month, per German rental convention.

## Common problems & issues

- **The Schufa/no-credit-history catch-22:** newcomers without German banking or credit history frequently get passed over even with a good income, because landlords default to trusting a Schufa score they don't have. Foreign bank statements and reference letters help but aren't universally accepted.
- **Landlord discrimination:** forum and advocacy reports (including German tenant-rights discussions) describe non-German-sounding names or nationality getting fewer callbacks; this is illegal under the Allgemeines Gleichbehandlungsgesetz (AGG) but hard to prove or enforce in practice. An LMU Munich study and the late-2025 National Discrimination and Racism Monitor (NaDiRa) housing report both found applicants with non-Western "ethnic" names — especially those read as Black or Muslim — are systematically less likely to even get a viewing, independent of income or documents.
- **National housing shortage context:** the Pestel Institute has estimated a nationwide shortage of 700,000+ dwellings (Germany's largest housing deficit in over 20 years), and InterNations' *Expat Insider 2025* survey found 63% of expats rate finding housing as hard, versus 36% globally — Nuremberg's competitive market sits inside this larger national squeeze, not an isolated local problem.
- **Massenbesichtigung competition:** popular listings routinely draw 20-40+ applicants at a single viewing slot, making the process feel like a job interview with no guarantee of a callback.
- **Fake/scam listings:** stolen photos, "landlord is abroad" stories asking for deposits before any viewing, requests for passport scans and bank details unusually early, pressure tactics ("decide within 2 hours"), and requests to pay via Western Union, crypto, or gift cards are all documented scam patterns. Never pay before signing a contract and physically viewing (or having a trusted person view) the unit.
- **Kitchen confusion:** most unfurnished German apartments have zero kitchen installed; outgoing tenants often sell their fitted kitchen (Einbauküche) separately, sometimes at inflated prices — confirm in writing whether a kitchen is included.
- **Utility bill surprises:** annual Nebenkosten settlements (Betriebskostenabrechnung) frequently result in additional payments owed rather than refunds — budget for this.
- **Slow Wohnungsgeberbestätigung:** landlords sometimes delay issuing this confirmation, which then delays your Anmeldung and everything downstream (bank account, health insurance, tax ID).
- **Building land shortage / undersupply:** Nuremberg has had comparatively low new-construction rates versus population growth for years, keeping the market tight, especially in central and northern districts.

## Benefits & drawbacks by search strategy

**ImmoScout24**
- Benefits: largest inventory, good filters, widely trusted, works for all budgets.
- Drawbacks: intense competition on desirable listings; some scam listings slip through.

**WG-Gesucht**
- Benefits: best for shared flats and temporary/bridge housing; large expat and student user base; often faster turnaround for smaller rooms.
- Drawbacks: paid membership often needed to compete for good listings; more geared to shared living than full family apartments.

**eBay Kleinanzeigen**
- Benefits: private landlords, sometimes lighter documentation requirements, occasionally better prices.
- Drawbacks: higher scam risk, less structured process, fewer legal protections in how listings are presented.

**Facebook groups**
- Benefits: word-of-mouth listings never posted elsewhere; can build community trust.
- Drawbacks: unstructured, no vetting, scams also circulate here.

**wbg Nürnberg / WBS subsidized housing**
- Benefits: significantly cheaper rent than market rate; strong tenant protections; good option for lower-income households, students, pensioners, or trainees.
- Drawbacks: requires qualifying under income limits and obtaining a Wohnberechtigungsschein (WBS) first, which itself takes time; waiting lists can be long; limited availability of units.

**Temporary/serviced housing (Wunderflats, Mr. Lodge, extended-stay)**
- Benefits: move-in ready, no Schufa required, good bridge solution while you build local paperwork.
- Drawbacks: 40-60% price premium over comparable unfurnished long-term rent; not sustainable long-term.

## Nuremberg-specific tips / office & portal links

- **wbg Nürnberg GmbH** — city housing association, market-rate and subsidized flats: https://wbg.nuernberg.de/en/rent/flats/subsidised-flats
- **WBS application / Amt für Existenzsicherung und soziale Integration**, Marienstraße 6, Nürnberg — apply for the Wohnberechtigungsschein here before searching subsidized listings: https://www.nuernberg.de/internet/sozialamt/wohnungsvermittlung_antrag.html
- **WBS eligibility calculator:** wbs-rechner.de/wohnberechtigungsschein-nuernberg
- Set filters on ImmoScout24/Immowelt for specific Nuremberg districts: Altstadt, Gostenhof, Südstadt (more affordable), Erlenstegen and Mögeldorf (quieter, pricier, northern).
- Nuremberg's international business community (Siemens, Adidas, etc.) means more English-friendly landlords and agents than in smaller Bavarian towns, but bring a German-speaking friend or translator to review the actual Mietvertrag — contracts are legally binding in German regardless of any English summary offered.
- Consult the **Deutscher Mieterbund** (German Tenants' Association) or its local Nuremberg branch if you have contract disputes or feel a clause (e.g., excessive deposit demand) is unlawful.
