# City Registration (Anmeldung) in Nuremberg

> Last researched: 2026-07-23. Verify current rules/fees before relying on this, as German bureaucracy changes frequently.

## Overview

Anmeldung is Germany's mandatory address registration with the local authority. Everyone living in Germany — German citizens and foreign nationals alike, regardless of visa type (EU citizen, work visa, EU Blue Card, job seeker visa, student visa, family reunification, freelance visa) — must register their residential address with the Bürgeramt within **14 days** of moving into permanent accommodation. This is a national requirement under the Bundesmeldegesetz (Federal Registration Act), though the exact appointment system and office locations are handled locally — in Nuremberg, that's the **Bürgeramt** network (e.g., Bürgeramt Mitte).

This step is the linchpin of settling in Germany: almost nothing else — opening a bank account, getting statutory or private health insurance, receiving your tax ID (Steuer-ID), booking your residence permit appointment at the Ausländerbehörde, registering a phone contract — works without the Meldebescheinigung (registration certificate) you get at the end of Anmeldung.

## Step-by-step process

1. **Secure a lease and get moving-in confirmed** — you need a real address to register (see the accommodation wiki page). If you're in temporary housing, you may still be able to register there if the host/landlord provides the required confirmation, but check with the Bürgeramt if the situation is unusual (e.g., staying with friends/family, short sublets).
2. **Request the Wohnungsgeberbestätigung from your landlord immediately after signing the lease or moving in.** Under § 19 of the Bundesmeldegesetz, landlords are legally obligated to provide this confirmation; in Nuremberg it can be filled out on paper or submitted online via the city's portal (mein.nuernberg.de / BayernPortal). Ask in writing and follow up if it's delayed — this is one of the most common bottlenecks.
3. **Book a Termin (appointment) online** at https://nuernberg.termine-reservieren.de/ (or via the Bürgeramt Mitte page: https://www.nuernberg.de/internet/buergeramt_mitte/termine.html). Select "Anmeldung," your preferred Bürgeramt location, and an available slot.
   - New appointment slots (up to 14 days ahead) are typically released on weekdays at **6:30 a.m.**
   - Same-day cancellation slots are often released around **7-8 a.m.**, with an additional release around 10 a.m. on Tuesdays.
   - If nothing is available, you can submit a **Terminwunsch** (appointment request); the system will offer up to three proposed slots by email, each valid for 24 hours to accept.
   - Alternatively, call the Service-Center at **0911/231-0** for help booking, especially if you lack reliable internet access.
   - Walk-in slots without an appointment exist for urgent cases on **Wednesdays 8 a.m.-12 p.m.** at Bürgeramt Mitte, Äußere Laufer Gasse 25 (verify current walk-in hours before relying on this).
4. **Gather your documents** (see checklist below) before the appointment.
5. **Attend your appointment.** Show your appointment confirmation (email with QR code/appointment number on your smartphone is sufficient — no printout strictly required, though bringing one as backup is sensible) to security at the entrance, check in at a terminal, then complete the registration desk process, which typically takes 15-20 minutes.
6. **Receive your Meldebescheinigung (Anmeldebestätigung) immediately** at the end of the appointment — this is your proof of registration.
7. **Use your Meldebescheinigung to unlock next steps**: open a German bank account, apply for statutory/private health insurance, wait for your Steueridentifikationsnummer (tax ID) to arrive by post from the Bundeszentralamt für Steuern (typically within 2-4 weeks), and — if you're a non-EU visa holder — book your residence permit appointment at the Ausländerbehörde (foreigners' office), which usually requires a registered address as a prerequisite.

## Documents/Requirements checklist

- Valid passport or national ID card (all household members being registered)
- **Wohnungsgeberbestätigung** — landlord/property owner confirmation of move-in (mandatory, per § 19 Bundesmeldegesetz); not required only if you are yourself the property owner
- Completed **Anmeldeformular** (registration form) — available in advance on nuernberg.de/BayernPortal, or filled out at the office
- Visa/residence permit documents, if applicable (non-EU nationals)
- Marriage certificate, if registering as a married couple/household
- Birth certificates for any children being registered
- Rental contract (Mietvertrag) as backup, though usually not strictly required if the Wohnungsgeberbestätigung is complete

## Costs (EUR, verify current figures)

- **Anmeldung itself is free** — no fee for standard address registration.
- **Wohnungsgeberbestätigung**: free of charge from the landlord/city portal.
- Registering a **secondary residence** (Zweitwohnsitz) may trigger a local secondary-residence tax in some municipalities — verify with the Nuremberg Bürgeramt whether this applies to your situation.
- No cost for the Meldebescheinigung issued on the spot; additional certified copies, if needed later, may carry a small administrative fee (verify current figures).

## Timeline

- **14 days** from move-in is the legal deadline to register (per the Bundesmeldegesetz).
- In Nuremberg, appointments are generally available within about 14 days across the various Bürgeramt locations, though desirable time slots can fill quickly — check daily around 6:30 a.m. release time if you're struggling to find a slot.
- Meldebescheinigung is issued the same day, at the appointment.
- Steuer-ID typically arrives by post 2-4 weeks after registration.
- Missing the 14-day window can result in a fine of up to **€1,000** under § 54 Bundesmeldegesetz, though in practice authorities tend to be lenient with newcomers who are clearly trying to comply and were blocked by appointment availability rather than negligence.

## Common problems & issues

- **Appointment system overload:** Nuremberg's booking portal releases limited daily slots at set times (6:30 a.m. for future dates, ~7-8 a.m. for same-day), and popular slots can disappear within minutes — a frequent complaint among newcomers is "no appointments for weeks."
- **Landlord delays on the Wohnungsgeberbestätigung:** some landlords are slow or unfamiliar with the requirement; request it in writing immediately after signing/moving in to create a paper trail if you need to escalate.
- **Chained bureaucracy delay:** because Anmeldung gates so much else (bank account, health insurance, tax ID, residence permit appointment), a slow Anmeldung appointment cascades into delays for everything downstream — plan for this early, ideally the same week you move in.
- **Language barrier at the desk:** while Nuremberg's larger international business presence means somewhat more English support than smaller towns, service can still default to German; bringing a German-speaking friend, translated document copies, or a translation app is commonly recommended.
- **Missing the 14-day deadline while waiting for appointment availability:** many people can't get a slot within the legal 14 days despite genuinely trying — document your attempts (e.g., screenshots of a fully booked calendar) in case you need to explain the delay.
- **Confusion between Anmeldung, Ummeldung, and Abmeldung:** newcomers occasionally aren't sure which one applies to their situation, especially when they've had temporary housing before their "real" lease.

## Ummeldung (re-registration when moving within the city or country)

- If you move to a new address within Nuremberg (or elsewhere in Germany), you must file a new registration (Ummeldung) within 14 days of the move, following essentially the same process as the original Anmeldung — book a Termin, bring your Wohnungsgeberbestätigung for the new address, passport, and old Meldebescheinigung if you have it.
- Your updated address is expected to filter through to your employer, bank, and tax office, but proactively notifying them directly is recommended rather than assuming automatic propagation.

## Abmeldung (deregistration when leaving)

- Before permanently leaving Germany (moving abroad), you must submit a deregistration (Abmeldung) at the Bürgeramt, generally allowed between **one week before and two weeks after** your departure date (verify current window with Nuremberg's Bürgeramt).
- Skipping Abmeldung leaves you registered indefinitely, which can mean continued liability for things like broadcasting fees (Rundfunkbeitrag), church tax, or insurance obligations tied to your registered address.
- Some cities now allow Abmeldung online; check nuernberg.de for the current digital option before assuming you must go in person.

## Benefits

- Unlocks essentially all further settling-in steps: bank account, health/public insurance enrollment, tax ID, mobile contracts, and (for non-EU visa holders) the residence permit appointment at the Ausländerbehörde.
- The Meldebescheinigung is a simple, official proof of address accepted everywhere in German bureaucracy.
- The process itself, once you have a slot and documents, is quick — typically 15-20 minutes at the desk.
- No fee for the core registration service.

## Drawbacks

- Appointment scarcity in a fast-growing city can mean weeks of checking the booking portal daily.
- Entirely dependent on landlord cooperation for the Wohnungsgeberbestätigung — a single uncooperative or slow landlord can block your entire timeline.
- The 14-day legal deadline creates real anxiety when appointment slots are unavailable through no fault of your own.
- Bureaucratic inflexibility: standard desk hours, in-person requirement (in most cases), and limited English-language support at the counter.

## Nuremberg-specific tips / office locations & booking links

- **Main appointment booking portal:** https://nuernberg.termine-reservieren.de/ (select "Anmeldung" and your nearest Bürgeramt)
- **Bürgeramt Mitte:** Äußere Laufer Gasse 25, Nürnberg — https://www.nuernberg.de/internet/buergeramt_mitte/
- **Bürgeramt Mitte appointment page:** https://www.nuernberg.de/internet/buergeramt_mitte/termine.html
- **Service-Center phone line for booking help:** 0911/231-0
- **Wohnungsgeberbestätigung online submission (for landlords) and city guidance:** available via mein.nuernberg.de and the BayernPortal (search "Wohnungsgeberbestätigung Nürnberg")
- Nuremberg has **multiple Bürgeramt locations** across the city, not just Bürgeramt Mitte — check the booking portal for the location with the earliest available slot near you, since wait times can differ significantly by office.
- Try checking the booking portal right at the daily 6:30 a.m. release window (and the ~7-8 a.m. same-day release) for the best chance at a near-term slot.
- If you cannot get a Termin before your 14-day deadline, keep evidence of your attempts (screenshots of the fully-booked calendar) in case you need to explain the delay to officials later.
