# Public Transport in Nuremberg (VGN)

> Last researched: 2026-07-23. Verify current rules/fees before relying on this, as German bureaucracy changes frequently.

**Sequence:** Side quest — unlocked after Anmeldung (useful from day one, but ticket subscriptions typically need a German bank account).
**Prerequisites:** [04-city-registration-anmeldung.md](04-city-registration-anmeldung.md) (helpful, not required, for a Deutschlandticket subscription), [05-bank-account.md](05-bank-account.md) (for SEPA-based subscriptions).
**Unlocks:** Practical daily mobility around Nuremberg, Fürth, Erlangen, and the wider metro area without a car — pairs with [11-car-buying-and-registration.md](11-car-buying-and-registration.md) as the alternative.

## Overview

Nuremberg's public transport is run under **VGN** (Verkehrsverbund Großraum Nürnberg), the transit association covering Nuremberg and its wider metro area (Fürth, Erlangen, and beyond), integrating U-Bahn (subway), S-Bahn (regional/city trains), trams, and buses under one ticketing system, operated locally by **VAG** (Verkehrs-Aktiengesellschaft Nürnberg).

## Process

- Buy tickets via the VGN Fahrplan & Tickets app, ticket vending machines at stations, or the VGN online shop.
- One ticket typically covers transfers across U-Bahn/S-Bahn/tram/bus within the relevant fare zone — no need to buy separate tickets per mode.
- The nationwide **Deutschlandticket** is valid on almost all local and regional public transport across Germany (not high-speed ICE/IC trains), including the full VGN network, and is a subscription that can be cancelled monthly.

## Costs (verify current figures)

- Deutschlandticket: €49/month (nationwide, subscription, monthly cancellable) — verify current price, as this has changed since introduction and may again.
- VGN single tickets, day tickets (TagesTicket Solo/Plus), and MobiCard weekly/monthly passes are available for those who don't want a subscription; day tickets and short-trip tickets are worth it for occasional riders.

## Common problems & issues

- Ticket inspectors (often plain-clothed) do random checks; riding without a valid, correctly-zoned ticket ("Schwarzfahren") results in an on-the-spot fine regardless of intent — a common newcomer mistake is validating the wrong ticket type or forgetting to validate a paper ticket before boarding.
- The Deutschlandticket doesn't cover long-distance/high-speed trains, which trips up people assuming it's an all-purpose rail pass.

## Benefits

- Single ticket/subscription covers every mode (U-Bahn, S-Bahn, tram, bus) across Nuremberg and the surrounding Franconia region.
- The Deutschlandticket is nationwide, monthly-cancellable, and inexpensive relative to owning a car.
- No German bank account or Schufa needed for single/day tickets bought on the spot — accessible from day one.

## Drawbacks

- Zone/ticket-type confusion is a common (and costly) newcomer mistake, since fines apply regardless of intent.
- Deutschlandticket doesn't help for regional trips on high-speed/long-distance trains.
- Off-peak/late-night frequency outside the city core can be limited compared to car ownership.

## Tips

- The VGN app is the easiest way to buy/manage tickets and check real-time departures.
- If you commute daily within the VGN area, the Deutschlandticket is usually the best value; for occasional use, day/single tickets may be cheaper.
