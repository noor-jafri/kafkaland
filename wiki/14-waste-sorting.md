# Waste Separation (Mülltrennung)

> Last researched: 2026-07-23. Verify current rules/fees before relying on this, as German bureaucracy changes frequently.

**Sequence:** Side quest — ongoing, starts the moment you move into a flat.
**Prerequisites:** [03-accommodation.md](03-accommodation.md).
**Unlocks:** Avoiding fines and neighbor/landlord friction.

## Overview

Germany's household waste sorting system is notoriously confusing for newcomers — rules, bin colors, and accepted items can vary by city/district, and getting it wrong can result in fines.

## Process

- Common bin categories: **blue** (Papier/paper & cardboard), **yellow** or yellow bag (Verpackungen/packaging — plastics, metals, composite materials), **brown** (Bioabfall/organic waste), **black/grey** (Restmüll/residual waste that doesn't fit elsewhere), and separate **glass containers** (sorted by color: clear, green, brown) usually at public collection points rather than at your building.
- Nuremberg's waste management is handled by the city's Abfallwirtschaftsbetrieb (AWB Nürnberg); check its website/app for an Abfall-ABC (waste lookup tool) where you can search almost any item to find the correct bin.
- Bottles/cans with a deposit (Pfand) are returned to supermarket reverse-vending machines for a cash refund rather than thrown away or recycled curbside.

## Costs (verify current figures)

- Waste collection fees are usually bundled into your Nebenkosten (utility costs) as a tenant rather than billed separately.
- Fines for incorrect sorting reported by other sources range roughly **€10–1,500** depending on severity and municipality — verify current fine schedule for Nuremberg specifically via AWB Nürnberg or the Ordnungsamt.

## Common problems & issues

- New arrivals frequently mis-sort items in the first weeks simply because they don't know the local color-to-category mapping, which can differ from other German cities or their home country's system entirely.
- Shared building bins mean the whole building can be flagged/fined if the Restmüll bin is contaminated with recyclables — neighbor complaints to the Ordnungsamt (regulatory office) are a common trigger for enforcement or a warning letter from the landlord/Hausverwaltung.
- Bulky waste (Sperrmüll) and electronics have separate disposal/pickup rules and can't just go in a normal bin.

## Benefits

- Once you know your building's color-to-bin mapping, ongoing effort is minimal — a habit, not a chore.
- Pfand (deposit) return on bottles/cans effectively pays you back a small amount for correct recycling.

## Drawbacks

- The learning curve is real, and mistakes in shared bins can affect (and annoy) an entire building, not just you.
- Rules aren't standardized nationally — knowledge from another German city or your home country can actively mislead you here.

## Tips

- Check AWB Nürnberg's site/app early for exact bin colors and pickup schedules for your address — don't assume it matches what you learn from generic guides written for other cities.
- When in doubt, don't guess — ask a neighbor or your landlord; a five-minute question avoids a fine or a passive-aggressive note taped to your door.
