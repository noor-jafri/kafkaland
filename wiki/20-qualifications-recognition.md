# Recognising Your Qualifications (Anerkennung)

> Last researched: 2026-07-23. Verify current rules/fees before relying on this, as German bureaucracy changes frequently.

**Sequence:** Side quest — do this early if your profession is regulated; it gates employment for some routes.
**Prerequisites:** None bureaucratically, but pairs with [01-before-you-arrive.md](01-before-you-arrive.md) (start the anabin check before you even land) and gates [21-job-search.md](21-job-search.md) for regulated professions.
**Unlocks:** [21-job-search.md](21-job-search.md) at your real skill level instead of a deskilled one.

## Overview

**Anerkennung** (recognition) is the process of having Germany formally evaluate your foreign qualification. For some jobs it's **mandatory**; for others it's optional but powerful. Getting this right is the single biggest defence against underemployment — Germany's over-qualification rate for non-EU workers sits just under 33%, and fewer than four in ten non-EU academics graduated abroad end up in a job matching their formal qualification.

### Regulated vs. non-regulated professions

| | Regulated professions | Non-regulated professions |
|---|---|---|
| **Examples** | Doctor, nurse, teacher, lawyer, engineer (in some cases), many crafts | Most IT, business, science, and many academic roles |
| **Recognition** | **Mandatory** — you legally can't practise without it | **Optional** — but helps employers trust your qualification |
| **Process** | Formal equivalence assessment, sometimes exams | anabin check / ZAB Statement of Comparability often enough |

## Step-by-step process

1. **Check anabin** — Germany's official database (run by the ZAB) rating foreign institutions/degrees (H+, H+/-, H-). Do this before relying on any other step.
2. **Use anerkennung-in-deutschland.de** to find the competent recognition authority for your profession and Bundesland (for Bavaria/Nuremberg, this is often the IHK Nürnberg für Mittelfranken for vocational/business qualifications, or the relevant state ministry for regulated professions like medicine and teaching).
3. **For non-regulated academic roles:** order a **ZAB Zeugnisbewertung** (Statement of Comparability) — a formal evaluation of your foreign higher-education degree, widely accepted by employers.
4. **For regulated roles:** start the formal equivalence process months ahead of any job search, and plan for language exams alongside it.
5. **If you already have a job offer:** ask your employer about a **Recognition Partnership (Anerkennungspartnerschaft)** — since March 2024, this lets skilled workers start working while recognition is still pending, under an agreement with the employer.

### Example: doctors (Approbation)

Recognition for regulated professions is heavy. For doctors specifically:
- You need the **Approbation** (licence to practise).
- Requires proof of equivalence of your medical degree, plus C1-level general German and a medical-language exam (**Fachsprachprüfung**).
- Processing typically runs 4–12 months, and requirements vary by Bundesland.
- If your training is judged only partially equivalent, you may face a **Kenntnisprüfung** (knowledge exam).

Nurses, teachers, and lawyers face similarly demanding, state-specific paths.

## Documents/Requirements checklist

- Degree certificates and transcripts (with certified translations if not in German/English)
- anabin lookup result for your institution/degree
- ZAB Zeugnisbewertung application (non-regulated roles) or formal equivalence application (regulated roles)
- Proof of relevant work experience (especially for IT specialists without a formal degree route)
- Language certificates (German, and sometimes English)
- For regulated health/legal professions: additional exam registrations (Fachsprachprüfung, Kenntnisprüfung)

## Costs (EUR, verify current figures)

- ZAB Zeugnisbewertung: typically in the low hundreds of euros — verify current fee on anerkennung-in-deutschland.de.
- Regulated-profession equivalence assessments and exams vary significantly by profession and state; medical Approbation processes can run into the thousands once exams and language certification are included.

## Timeline

- anabin/ZAB checks: days to a few weeks once documents are submitted.
- Regulated-profession recognition (e.g. Approbation): commonly 4–12 months, longer if a knowledge exam is required.
- Start this in parallel with your visa application (see [01-before-you-arrive.md](01-before-you-arrive.md)) if your profession is regulated — it has one of the longest lead times in the whole process.

## Common problems & issues

- **Deskilling risk:** the emphasis on formal recognition means real skills can be discounted unless documented the German way. A 2024 KOFA study found around 520,000 migrant women unemployed in Germany, many overqualified for the work available to them.
- **State-by-state variation:** regulated-profession requirements differ by Bundesland, so generic advice online can be wrong for your specific case — always confirm with the Bavaria-specific competent authority.
- **Underestimating language requirements:** especially for medicine and law, the language bar (often C1) is as big a hurdle as the technical equivalence check.
- **Assuming recognition is unnecessary:** even in non-regulated fields, skipping a ZAB Zeugnisbewertung can mean employers discount or misjudge a foreign degree during hiring.

## Benefits

- Formal recognition is the strongest available defence against underemployment/deskilling.
- The Recognition Partnership (since March 2024) lets you start earning while the paperwork is still pending, rather than waiting months with no income.
- Once granted, recognition is permanent and portable across German employers.

## Drawbacks

- Regulated-profession recognition is slow, state-specific, and can require re-sitting exams even after years of foreign practice.
- Costs and lead time can be substantial for medicine, law, and other heavily regulated fields.
- The process is easy to underestimate or start too late, since it isn't gated by a single obvious appointment the way Anmeldung is.

## Nuremberg-specific tips

- **IHK Nürnberg für Mittelfranken** handles recognition of many vocational/commercial (non-academic) qualifications for the Nuremberg region — check ihk-nuernberg.de for the current process.
- For academic degree evaluation, the ZAB process is centralised nationally (not city-specific), so the Zeugnisbewertung applies the same way regardless of where in Germany you settle.
- Regulated-profession authorities (medicine, law, teaching) are typically organized at the Bavarian state level (e.g., Regierung von Mittelfranken for some health professions) rather than the city — check anerkennung-in-deutschland.de's authority finder for the exact Bavarian office for your profession.
